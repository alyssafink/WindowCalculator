The key difference between single-pane and double-pane windows is that single-pane windows are made of a single sheet of glass, while double-pane windows are made of two sheets of glass that are separated by an air gap.
If you look where the glass meets the frame, sometimes you can see whether there are one or two sheets of glass. If you can't tell or aren't sure, the reflection method can help. Try holding a small object close to the glass, like a pen or pencil. Look closely at the glass -- if you can see one reflection, it's a single-pane window, and if you see two reflections, it's a double-pane window. This YouTube video walks you through the process.
If you aren't the first owner of your house, you might consider checking each window, since they aren't always all replaced at the same time.

Homes are made up of a lot of different parts, and each one is as unique as the people who live in it. This means that calculating things like energy consumption requires a lot of information. Read below to learn how the Window Calculator simplifies this calculation process across five different topics in order to generate a personalized report for your home.
The amount of energy that a building uses is determined by both the way that heat flows through it and the electricity used by appliances and lighting. When more heat is entering a building than it's releasing, a cooling system will turn on and use energy to lower the temperature back to a comfortable level.
The Window Calculator uses typical values about parts of your home (like the insulating ability of different window types) information about the physics of heat flow and to determine how much energy the three upgrade options save by reducing heat loss in the winter and heat gain in the summer.
The cost of window upgrades will vary based on a lot of factors, including base window size, quantity, quality, installation complexity, brand, installer, labor costs, permitting, rebates, and more. In order to estimate a reasonable total expected cost for each upgrade option, the Window Calculator relies on information from manufacturers and installers to create a range of potential costs for a typical project, based on the total number and size of the windows considered for upgrades.
Embodied carbon is one measurement of the environmental impact that a product has before it is installed in your home, including manufacturing and transportation. The Window Calculator cross-references the size of your windows with third-party verified data about the embodied carbon of all three upgrade options to estimate the environmental impact of each option before it enters your home.
Upgrading your windows can have a big impact on the comfort of your home, helping it feel cooler in the summer and warmer in the winter. This is because thermal comfort depends on both the air temperature and the temperature of things around you. The Window Calculator uses information about the typical insulating and shading properties of different window types to determines how hot your windows will get in the summer. This is then used to calculate how much hotter your windows make your home feel.
Windows are often a weak point in the effort to keep unwanted noise from airplanes, barking dogs, and loud neighbors out of your home. The Window Calculator compares the typical sound insulating properties of single-pane windows with each window upgrade option to determine how much they improve your privacy.
